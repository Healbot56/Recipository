Black Forest Gateau

Ingredients

Note: Mass is the preferred measure. Volumetric measurements are reference only.

Ingredient Amount Note
Salted butter 175g
Dark chocolate bar 200g
Plain flour 300g
Sugar 375g
Cocoa 25g
Bicarbonate of soda 1 tsp Baking soda
Medium eggs 2
Buttermilk 200g Or natural yogurt

Instructions

Preparation for baking

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Grease the base of 3 20cm cake tins. (We use 2x cake tins of unknown diameter)
  3. Line the tins with baking parchment.

Section 1 - Creating chocolate mixture

  1. Boil the kettle
  2. Put the butter and 75g of chocolate, broken into chunks, into a small pan and gently heat, while stirring, until completely melted.

Section 2 - Making cake batter

  1. Mix together the flour, sugar, cocoa, and bicarbonate of soda with a pinch of salt in a mixing bowl.
  2. Whisk the eggs and buttermilk (or yogurt if substituted) together.
  3. Scrape the melted chocolate mixture and the egg mixture into the dry Ingredients.
  4. Add 100ml boiling water and mix/whisk briefly with an electric whisk until the cake batter is lump free.

Section 3 - Baking the cakes

  1. Divide the mixture between the tins.
  2. Bake for 25 minutes, swapping the tins after 20 minutes if they're on different shelves.
  3. To test if they're done, push in a skewer and check that it comes out clean.

Section 4 - After-baking cake operations

  1. Prick the cakes with a skewer. Note: The pricking should be done shallowly and repeatedly over the entire surface of the cakes in order to prepare the surface to accept the kirsch.
  2. Mix together 2 tbsp of cherry juice and the kirsch (or more juice instead).
  3. Drizzle the mixture over the cakes.
  4. Cool the cakes.

Section 5 - Assembling the cakes

  1. Mix together the remaining drained cherries and jam. Tip 200ml of the cream into a small pan and heat until just below simmering point.
  2. Chop the remaining chocolate and put in a heatproof bowl.
  3. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and stir until melted.

NOTE: We instead put the chocolate into a pot, add the cream, and heat on the stove.
